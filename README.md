# IndustrialRevCYOA
IR CYOA 1
Game Design Document
Smoke, Steam and Survival
A Year 8 Industrial Revolution CYOA Learning Game
1. Purpose

This game teaches Year 8 students how the Industrial Revolution changed Britain through farming, inventions, factories, cities, transport, class, child labour and reform.

Students experience industrialisation through choices, consequences and historical evidence.

2. Target audience

Year 8 History / HASS students.

Reading level should be clear, direct and accessible. Sentences should usually be short. Difficult terms should have glossary support.

3. Core question

Was the Industrial Revolution progress, suffering, or both?

4. Player role

The player chooses one character:

Factory Child
Focus: child labour, factory discipline, wages, injury, reform.
Rural Farmer’s Child
Focus: enclosure, agricultural change, movement to cities.
Apprentice Engineer
Focus: inventions, steam power, transport, machinery.
Mill Owner’s Child
Focus: profit, investment, wages, responsibility, reform pressure.
Young Reformer
Focus: evidence, petitions, public health, factory laws.
5. Game stats

Each choice changes five stats:

Stat	Meaning
Money	Survival, wages, savings
Health	Safety, disease, exhaustion
Skills	Knowledge, education, technical ability
Reputation	Trust, respect, social standing
Reform	Contribution to improving society

Starting stats:

{
  "money": 2,
  "health": 3,
  "skills": 1,
  "reputation": 1,
  "reform": 0
}
6. Main chapters
Life before factories
Enclosure and farming change
The journey to Manchester
Factory work
Machines and inventions
Steam, coal and iron
Urban life and disease
Transport revolution
Protest and reform
Final judgement
7. Required app features

The app should include:

title screen
role selection
story scene display
three choice buttons per scene
stat tracker
glossary pop-ups
historical note after each choice
evidence notebook
restart button
local save
final reflection screen
export or copy final response
8. Evidence notebook

Students collect evidence during the game.

Evidence examples:

Evidence	Historical idea
Enclosure forced many poor families off common land	Agricultural Revolution
Cotton mills employed women and children	Factory system
Steam engines allowed factories to grow away from rivers	Steam power
Manchester grew rapidly and became polluted	Urbanisation
Factory Acts tried to limit abuses	Reform
9. End task

At the end, students answer:

Was the Industrial Revolution mainly positive, mainly negative, or mixed?

They must use at least three pieces of evidence from their notebook.
